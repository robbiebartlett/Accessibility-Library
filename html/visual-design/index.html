Visual design

Accessible visual design ensures as many people as possible will have access to our work.

Have you ever misplaced your glasses or attempted to read a screen with sunlight glare? You may have benefited from accessibility features including enlarged text or high-contrast colours.

The diverse nature of impairments creates variations in how people perceive design.

Someone who is colour-blind should still be able to differentiate between distinct elements. The fonts, too, should be easy to read.

It also helps others who find themselves with temporary or situational limitations.

A clean and clear visual presentation helps make sense of information and functionality.
