Accessibility auditing

Does your digital product or service meet the NZ Government Web Accessibility Standard?

The Accessibility Team audits websites to see if they have barriers that may affect disabled people. The Accessibility Team also provides informal advice on accessibility.

What do I need to provide for an audit?

Before contacting the team check which elements you want the audit to include. Consider the pages that are critical to the main user journeys people take to complete tasks.

The pages and components key to this sequence are the ones to consider for assessing. You should focus on:

  • providing a sample of your page templates and content types
  • any key interactive features
  • your most significant user journeys
  • any problematic areas you’ve seen during automated testing.

If you build any reusable components test them all. Consider using a library (e.g. New Zealand Government Design System) for long term benefits.

Accessibility Assurance Process

The Accessibility Team provides an accurate, easy to understand and comprehensive auditing process.

Sometimes, it is impossible to meet all these expectations to a high standard. To manage these expectations we provide an assurance process to produce quality results.

We define this assurance process in 'tiers', allowing for an efficient use of resources. A full description of the tier will be provided to the requestor at the beginning of the process.

Tier 1 – Comprehensive

Comprehensive auditing will provide the most accurate and comprehensive results. Comprehensive audits are recommended when websites are in the User Acceptance Testing (UAT) phase.

  • Auditing may take up to one month, depending on the size of the website
  • Audits will be peer-reviewed by another member of the Accessibility Team
  • Audits will be signed off by the Technical Product Owner (TPO) of the Accessibility Team.

Follow up

  • An Accessibility Statement will be issued by the Accessibility Team
  • The Accessibility Team Product owner (TPO) will request progress reports from all audits. Remediation progress will be assessed and reports provided to the Accessibility Management Team.

Tier 2 – Partial

Partial audits detect a moderate level of accessibility issues. These take less time than Tier 1 - Comprehensive audits.

Tier 2 audits are appropriate for websites that are in early stages of development. This is ideal for when a Comprehensive audit would be inappropriate due to rapidly changing web content.

This is also suited for audits that were not planned, where there is an urgent need for accessibility assurance.

  • Partial audits are recommended for all websites during development
  • A Partial audit may take up to 4 days
  • Partial audits will be peer-reviewed by another member of the Accessibility Team
  • Partial audits will be signed off by the Technical Product Owner (TPO) of the Accessibility Team after peer review has occurred.

Tier 3 – Advice

Tier 3 advice provides a high-level summary of potential accessibility issues.

Tier 3 is recommended when the request to audit is unexpected. Or if the requestor does not want a Comprehensive or Partial audit.

  • Tier 3 advice should take up to 1 day
  • Advice is not fully reliant to ensure web content is accessible
  • Advice may be provided as an informal email, screen recording, or phone call.

This assurance process also defines requirements for monitoring, reporting, and follow-up. Avoid leaving your audit until late in the project cycle, making sure there’s time to act on recommendations prior to launch.

If you have any questions or are preparing for an audit contact the Accessibility Team today.
